**Yes, drug conjugation can change an antibody's binding affinity**, though the extent of the change depends entirely on the conjugation method, the site of attachment, and the payload. While many carefully optimized antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs) preserve their parental antibody's exact target affinity, poorly managed conjugation can alter binding kinetics by **2- to 10-fold**. \[1, 2, 3, 4\]

Bioconjugation can impact binding affinity through several distinct mechanisms: \[5\]

## **1\. Location of the Conjugation Site**

The spatial placement of the payload is the most critical factor influencing binding: \[1, 6\]

* **Steric Hindrance:** If the linker-payload is attached near or directly within the **Complementarity-Determining Regions (CDRs)**—the variable regions responsible for antigen recognition—it can physically block the antibody from binding its target. \[4, 7, 8, 9\]  
* **Random vs. Site-Specific Chemistry:** Traditional "shotgun" techniques (e.g., random lysine conjugation) often attach payloads unpredictably across the entire structure. If a crucial lysine residue sits inside the antigen-binding pocket, affinity drops significantly. Modern methods prioritize site-specific engineering to ensure the payload attaches far away from the binding site. \[4, 7, 10, 11, 12\]

## **2\. Payload Hydrophobicity and Drug-to-Antibody Ratio (DAR)**

The chemical traits and volume of the attached payload directly influence structural behavior: \[13, 14\]

* **High DAR Negative Effects:** A high Drug-to-Antibody Ratio (e.g., DAR of 8, meaning eight drug molecules per antibody) can alter the antibody’s native conformation. \[15, 16, 17, 18\]  
* **Hydrophobic Aggregation:** Many cytotoxic payloads are highly hydrophobic. Loading too many hydrophobic molecules can cause structural distortion, misfolding, or micro-aggregation, reducing the antibody's functional capability to bind its antigen. \[13, 15, 19, 20, 21\]

## **3\. Allosteric and Structural Changes**

Modifying the constant region (Fc domain) of the antibody can induce subtle structural shifts across the entire protein chain. These remote chemical attachments can dynamically alter the shape of the distant antigen-binding pocket, unintentionally weakening or shifting its binding kinetics. \[1, 16\]

## ---

**Key Balancing Act in ADC Engineering**

| Conjugation Approach \[4, 7, 12, 13, 15, 16\] | Risk to Binding Affinity | Structural Impact |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Random Lysine / Cysteine** | **High** | Payloads attach arbitrarily, potentially inside or right next to the CDRs. |
| **Site-Specific (e.g., Glycan / Engineered Cys)** | **Low** | Payload is chemically restricted to a precise, remote zone far from the binding site. |
| **High Drug Load (DAR ≥ 8\)** | **Moderate to High** | Hydrophobic stress can trigger structural distortion or local aggregation. |

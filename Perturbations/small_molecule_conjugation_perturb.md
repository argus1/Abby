How Conjugation Perturbs Predictions

**Computational tools struggle with antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs) and similar molecules due to four major factors:**

* **Steric Hindrance:** The conjugated small molecule (the payload) and its linker are physically larger and more dynamic than the amino acid side chains they replace. Models like FoldX typically predict affinity for fixed, static interfaces. They cannot easily account for a bulky, moving payload blocking the path of the target antigen.  
* **Electrostatic and Hydrophobic Shifts:** Predicting binding relies heavily on the charge and water-attracting properties of amino acids. Adding a hydrophobic payload or a charged linker drastically changes local Solvated Interaction Energy ($SIE$), altering the solvation penalty—the energy required to strip water molecules away before binding.  
* **Conformational Entropy Changes:** Native antibodies hold specific structural shapes. Covalent attachment of small molecules limits or changes the ways these binding regions (Complementarity-Determining Regions, or CDRs) can flex and adapt to an antigen, increasing the entropic cost of binding.  
* **Conjugation Heterogeneity:** Traditional chemical modifications (like lysine or cysteine linking) attach payloads to multiple, random locations across the antibody structure. Because the placement and number of molecules (the Drug-to-Antibody Ratio or DAR) vary from antibody to antibody, computational docking produces a highly unpredictable average of affinity states. \[[1](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.bioconjchem.1c00313#:~:text=If%20regions%20close%20to%2C%20or%20within%2C%20the,with%20the%20antibody's%20capability%20to%20interact%20wit), [2](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5423484/#:~:text=ADCs%20with%20the%20DAR%20value%20of%20more,lead%20to%20solubility%20problem.%20Currently%2C%20the%20widel), [4](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0022354924001801#:~:text=Development%20of%20ADCs%20can%20become%20more%20challenging,potential%20alterations%20in%20the%20molecular%20surface%20properties), [13](https://www.mdpi.com/2227-9059/2/1/1#:~:text=Overall%2C%20in%20the%20case%20of%20conjugation%20through,at%20a%20relatively%20very%20few%20%CE%B5%2Damino%20grou), [14](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5032988/#:~:text=The%20distinct%20structures%20of%20these%20various%20molecular,varying%20number%20of%20drugs%20on%20each%20antibody%2C)\]

Real-World Example: Lysine and Thiol Modification

**In classical chemistry, small molecules are attached to naturally occurring, exposed amino acids on the antibody.**

* If you link the small molecule to a **lysine** (which carries a positive charge), you neutralize that charge and replace it with a hydrophobic or neutral linker-drug structure. Structure-based models predicting affinity will assume the original lysine charge is present, leading to an incorrect prediction of electrostatic attraction.

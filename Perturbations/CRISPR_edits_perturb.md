Key Perturbations in Structure-Based Prediction

**Structure-based tools (like Schrödinger Bioluminate or deep learning models) estimate binding strength based on 3D conformations. CRISPR edits perturb these predictions in several specific ways:**

* **Unintended Indels & Mismatches:** CRISPR double-strand breaks repaired by non-homologous end joining (NHEJ) often result in small insertions or deletions (indels) or frame-shifts. This corrupts the amino acid sequence, altering the overall volume, shape, and charge of the complementarity-determining regions (CDRs). Structure prediction models will miscalculate binding if they rely on the unedited, expected sequence.  
* **Protein Backbone Shifts:** When using CRISPR-based homology-directed repair (HDR) to swap or mutate antibody cassettes, the structural framework may buckle. Even sub-angstrom backbone shifts in the heavy or light chains disrupt the precise geometric positioning required for the antibody to bind its antigen.  
* **Disrupted Disulfide Bonds:** CRISPR edits in the constant or variable regions can inadvertently mutate conserved cysteines. If these are mutated, the expected disulfide bridges (the molecular "staples" that hold the antibody loops together) fail to form. Structure prediction models predicting folded, stable bonds will yield vastly incorrect affinity scores.  
* **Altered Glycosylation and PTMs:** Edits may unintentionally introduce or delete motifs (e.g., $N-X-S\\text{/}T$ motifs) that dictate sugar molecule attachment (glycosylation). Adding or removing these bulky, heavy sugar chains changes the local steric environment and blocks antigen accessibility, which is difficult to simulate accurately in static modeling. \[[13](https://www.schrodinger.com/life-science/solutions/antibody-design/#:~:text=Identify%20and%20prioritize%20promising%20leads%20by%20modeling,tools%20for%20structure%20characterization.%20Highlight%20potential%20su)\]

How Computational Tools Attempt to Adjust

To compensate for CRISPR-induced structural noise, advanced prediction platforms (such as the AntiFold Model) use inverse folding and probabilistic scoring. Rather than relying on a single static 3D structure, these tools evaluate ensembles of possible protein shapes and score how well different amino acid sequences fit into those shapes. \[[3](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11961221/#:~:text=Summary.%20The%20design%20and%20optimization%20of%20antibodies,Protein%20inverse%20folding%20models%2C%20capable%20of%20generati)\]

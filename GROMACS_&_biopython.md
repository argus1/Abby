GROMACS does not have a direct, native interface built into Biopython. Instead, GROMACS provides its own dedicated Python API called **gmxapi**. Biopython acts as a pre- and post-processing toolkit, whereas **gmxapi** is designed to execute and control simulations. \[[1](https://manual.gromacs.org/current/gmxapi/), [2](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35157693/#:~:text=Abstract.%20Gmxapi%20provides%20an%20integrated%2C%20native%20Python,simulations%20in%20GROMACS.%20The%20Python%20interface%20permit), [3](https://www.gromacs.org/topic/gmxapi-python.html#:~:text=gmxapi%20Python%20interface%20for%20GROMACS%20%E2%80%94%20GROMACS,interface%20for%20GROMACS.%20gmxapi%20allows%20molecular%20simula), [4](https://biopython.org/wiki/GSOC2010_Joao#:~:text=PDB%20is%20a%20smooth%20integration/interaction%20layer%20for,AutoDock%2C%20HADDOCK.%20It%20could%20be%20argued%20tha), [5](https://biopython.org/wiki/GSOC2010_Joao#:~:text=Instead%2C%20I%20believe%20it%20to%20be%20more,and%20AlignIO%20modules.%20This%2C%20together%20with%20the)\]

**Because Biopython's module handles the mining and parsing of structural data, it is often combined with GROMACS workflows. To bridge the two, the community relies on intermediary packages:**

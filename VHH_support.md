Nanobodies (also known as single-domain antibodies or VHHs) are **a unique class of compact antibody fragments derived from the heavy-chain-only antibodies naturally produced by camelids (like llamas and alpacas) and certain sharks**. Measuring only one-tenth the size of conventional antibodies, they bind to targets with equivalent specificity but offer far greater tissue penetration, chemical stability, and structural adaptability. \[[1](https://www.bosterbio.com/services/custom-antibody-production-services/nanobody-discovery-service-custom-vhh-antibodies/what#:~:text=What%20Is%20a%20Nanobody?%20Nanobodies%E2%80%94also%20known%20as,antigen%2Dbinding%20fragments%20derived%20from%20the%20heavy%2Dchain%2Donly%20antibo), [2](https://www.alcimed.com/en/insights/nanobodies/#:~:text=Nanobodies%2C%20also%20referred%20to%20as%20single%20domain,immunology%2C%20are%20single%2Ddomain%20antibody%20fragments%20derived%20fr), [3](https://isogenica.com/nanobody-vhh/#:~:text=Coining%20the%20term%20'Nanobody'%20Around%20the%20same,use%20beyond%20the%20lab.%20Ablynx%2C%20the%20Belgi), [4](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1359644625002375#:~:text=Nanobodies%20\(Nbs\)%20are%20genetically%20engineered%20single%20domain,\(HcAbs\)%20found%20in%20camelid%20species.%20These%20monomeric), [5](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12109276/#:~:text=Simple%20Summary.%20Nanobodies%2C%20derived%20from%20heavy%2Dchain%20antibodies,single%2Ddomain%20antibodies%20with%20unique%20structural%20and%20f)\]

**Here are a few key characteristics that define nanobodies:**

* **Size and Structure:** Traditional human or animal antibodies (IgG) consist of two heavy and two light chains. Nanobodies are simply the variable domains of heavy-chain-only antibodies. Because they lack a light chain, their molecular weight is roughly 15 kDa.  
* **The "Reach" Advantage:** Conventional antibodies bind using a flat or concave surface, whereas nanobodies often feature an extended binding loop that can easily reach into hidden, concave cavities of target proteins (like those on viruses or cancer cells) that are typically inaccessible.  
* **High Stability:** Nanobodies are highly robust; they can tolerate extreme temperatures, high pressures, and wide pH ranges much better than standard monoclonal antibodies.  
* **Clinical and Practical Applications:** Their unique profile makes them incredibly versatile. They easily cross biological barriers (including the blood-brain barrier). In medicine, they are used for targeted drug delivery, deep tissue imaging, and treating conditions such as severe blood disorders and cancer. \[[3](https://isogenica.com/nanobody-vhh/#:~:text=Coining%20the%20term%20'Nanobody'%20Around%20the%20same,use%20beyond%20the%20lab.%20Ablynx%2C%20the%20Belgi), [4](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1359644625002375#:~:text=Nanobodies%20\(Nbs\)%20are%20genetically%20engineered%20single%20domain,\(HcAbs\)%20found%20in%20camelid%20species.%20These%20monomeric), [5](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12109276/#:~:text=Simple%20Summary.%20Nanobodies%2C%20derived%20from%20heavy%2Dchain%20antibodies,single%2Ddomain%20antibodies%20with%20unique%20structural%20and%20f), [7](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10057852/#:~:text=Nanobodies%20\(Nbs\)%20are%20antibody%20fragments%20derived%20from,several%20advantages%20over%20conventional%20monoclonal%20antibodies%20\(mAbs), [14](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/medicine-and-dentistry/nanobody#:~:text=Nanobody.%20In%20subject%20area:%20Medicine%20and%20Dentistry.,antibodies%20known%20for%20their%20powerful%20binding%20and)\]

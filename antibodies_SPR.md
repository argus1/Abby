There is no universal dataset or single percentage tracking how many antibodies globally have Surface Plasmon Resonance (SPR) characterized binding. Instead, the percentage varies drastically—**near 100% for modern therapeutic antibodies**, but **only a tiny fraction (likely under 1%) for the broader pool of millions of commercial research antibodies**. \[1\]

The stark division across different sectors of biotechnology dictates how often this characterization takes place.

## **1\. Clinical and Therapeutic Antibodies (Nearly 100%)**

In pharmaceutical drug discovery (such as developing monoclonal antibodies or antibody-drug conjugates), **nearly 100% of candidate antibodies undergo SPR characterization**. \[2, 3\]

* **Regulatory Requirements:** Regulatory bodies like the FDA and EMA require exact binding kinetics—specifically the association rate ($k\_{on}$), dissociation rate ($k\_{off}$), and overall affinity ($K\_D$). \[4, 5\]  
* **Quality Control:** SPR is used as a critical quality control tool to measure batch-to-batch consistency and assess how chemical modifications (like conjugation) impact binding. A therapeutic antibody cannot move into clinical trials without this biophysical data. \[6, 7, 8, 9, 10\]

## **2\. Commercial Research Antibodies (A Small Fraction)**

For the millions of catalog antibodies sold for laboratory applications (like Western blots, IHC, or flow cytometry), **only a very small fraction are sold with exact SPR data**. \[1, 11\]

* **The "Antibody Reproducibility Crisis":** Historically, commercial vendors used simple, qualitative "yes/no" binding assays like ELISA rather than calculating exact kinetic constants. Published reviews note that a historical lack of rigorous validation meant up to 50% of commercial catalog antibodies failed to reliably bind their intended targets. \[1, 12, 13, 14\]  
* **Modern Shifts:** High-throughput SPR platforms (such as the Carterra LSA or multiplexed array systems) are beginning to change this. Leading catalog vendors have started systematically re-characterizing their top-selling antibodies using SPR to combat reproducibility issues, but the vast majority of the millions of niche research antibodies on the market still lack this premium data. \[1, 15, 16, 17, 18\]

## **Why Every Antibody Isn't Characterized by SPR**

While SPR is considered the gold standard for binding kinetics, two main bottlenecks historically limited its widespread use across all antibodies: \[19\]

* **Cost and Complexity:** Traditional SPR hardware (like Biacore systems) requires expensive gold-sensor chips, microfluidics, and highly specialized technicians to develop and validate individual assays. \[20, 21, 22, 23, 24\]  
* **Throughput Limitations:** Classic SPR instruments process only a few samples sequentially. While modern high-throughput systems can now screen up to thousands of antibody variants simultaneously, these technologies are concentrated in well-funded pharma pipelines and specialized core facilities rather than standard bulk manufacturing. \[15, 25\]
